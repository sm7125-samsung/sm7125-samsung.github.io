PREINSTALL INSTRUCTIONS

Unlock Bootloader

  1. Connect the device to a Wi-Fi network.
  2. Enable Developer Options by pressing the “Build Number” option in the “Settings” app within the “About” menu.
  3. From within the Developer options menu, enable OEM unlock.
  4. Connect USB cable to PC Power off the device, and boot it into download mode : With the device powered off, hold Volume Up + Volume Down
  5. Now, hold down the “Volume Up” button until you see the “Unlock bootloader” screen.
  6. Then press the “Volume Up” button once to unlock the bootloader.
  7. Your device will reboot twice and erase your data, you may now unplug the USB cable from your device.
  8. Run through Android Setup skipping everything you can, then connect the device to a Wi-Fi network.
  9. Re-enable Development settings by clicking the “Build Number” option 7 times, in the “Settings” app within the “About” menu, and verify that “OEM Unlock” is enabled and greyed out in the “Developer options” menu.

Patch VBMETA

  1. VBMETA is the partition that basically holds partition checksums that are verified during boot. It is a way to detect if any partitions have been modified by installing a custom recovery or a custom rom. This is why, if we want to boot we need to patch this.
  2. To patch VBMETA, download the tar file for your device from here.
  3. Boot into download mode following step 4 from above and then click the button that the onscreen instructions correlate to “Continue”.
  4. Select the patched VBMETA file you downloaded in the AP slot in odin and hit "Start".
